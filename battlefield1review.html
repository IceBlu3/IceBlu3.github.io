Battlefield 1 (Reviewed on PC by Ethan Jones, played for 55 hours)

Battlefield 1 is DICE's latest entry in the super popular Battlefield series. Unlike some of the previous entries such as Battlefield 3 and Battlefield 4, Battlefield 1 is set in World War 1. The setting change is refreshing, and extremley well exectued. When I first heard that the next Battlefield would be going to World War 1, I was a bit nervous that it would not turn out so great. I was completley wrong and the setting change is one of the best features ever added to any Battlefield. When it comes to Graphics Battlefield 1 is stunning, the graphics are amazing, and the map detail is superb. Unfortunetly there are more then a few graphical glitches, the main one being a glitch that ocurs to some PC users, such as myslef, where the sun on the map Aimens expands to cover half of the map. Battlefield 1 offers 6 modes: Conquest, Domination, Operations, Rush, War Pigeons, and Team Deathmatch. All of these are unique, and play well. My favorite mode so far is Operations, a new mode to the series. Operations provide a mode that feels like real war. It is a attack and defend game mode. The attackers have a certain number of lives and have to rush the defenders to take different sections of the map. Each Operation is in a playlist of two maps that correspond in story, so after one map is cleared by the attackers the defenders get another chance to defend and win on the next map.
Battlefied 1 has a 7 - 10 hour campaign that although short is very engaging and intertaining. It is told in little war stories, there are 6 of them and they can each be completed in 1 and a half to 2 hours each.
Overall besides it's glitches Battlefield 1 is almost the perfect shooter and the best I have seen in years.
Score: 9.8
